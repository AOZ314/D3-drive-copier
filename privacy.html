Privacy Policy

D3 — Duplicate Directories in Drive  ·  Last updated: 2025

D3 is a Google Apps Script web application that copies folder structures within Google Drive. This policy explains what data D3 accesses, how it is used, and what it does not do.

Scope of this policy: This policy covers the D3 source code itself and the official public deployment linked from the D3 GitHub repository. It does not cover copies of D3 deployed by other people — see the section below on third-party deployments.

What data D3 accesses

D3 requests access to your Google Drive in order to read the folders and files you select as a copy source, and to create copies at the destination you choose. It also reads your Google account email address to display which account is currently active in the application header.

How your data is used

D3 uses your Drive access solely to perform the copy operations you explicitly initiate. Your email address is displayed in the app interface only — it is not stored beyond your current session. Job progress and settings are stored in Google Apps Script's UserProperties service, which is scoped entirely to your Google account and is not accessible to anyone else.

Data sharing

D3 does not share your data with any third party. No data is transmitted to any external server, analytics service, or database run by the D3 project. All copy operations happen entirely within Google's infrastructure, inside your own Google account.

Google platform analytics

D3 runs on Google Apps Script, a platform operated by Google LLC. As part of operating that platform, Google automatically collects aggregate, anonymised usage data — such as the number of users accessing the app and error rates. This data is visible to the project owner in the Apps Script dashboard and is collected by Google, not by D3. No personal information is included. This collection is governed by Google's Privacy Policy and is standard for all Apps Script web apps.

Data storage

The only data D3 stores is job queue state (source/destination folder names and IDs, copy progress, settings) in Google Apps Script's UserProperties. This data lives in your Google account, is only accessible to you, and is automatically cleared when you use the "Reset all" function or revoke the app's authorization in your Google Account settings.

Third-party deployments

D3 is open-source and anyone can deploy their own copy. This policy does not cover deployments made by people other than the official maintainer. If you are using a version of D3 deployed by someone other than the official project, you are doing so at your own risk. We take no responsibility for how third parties may have modified the code or configured their deployment. If in doubt, deploy your own copy directly from the source repository — instructions are in the README.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
